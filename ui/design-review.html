My honest take

V2 — working baseline

Functional. Complete. Generic.

Has every feature, and that's a real asset. But visually it could be any other MOBA tool — which is the problem. The home page works hard with too many competing cards. Leans on win rates, which is what we're trying to move away from.

V3 — clean dashboard

Tidier than V2, but no point of view.

Same conventions, slightly cleaner. Doesn't earn its existence. Whichever direction you pick — it isn't this one. Retire it.

V4 — esports glass

Real personality, borrowed tropes.

The glass + gradients + clip-cut tiles are well executed. They're also what every esports tool from 2023 onward looks like. Cyan/violet says "futuristic gaming" without saying which game. The Build Lab proves the engine; the visual treatment slightly fights the precision. Strength: familiar, gaming-fluent. Risk: dates quickly, easy to mistake for any other tool.

V5 — editorial mustard

Distinctive point of view.

The editorial restraint reads curated, not auto-generated. Mustard on warm black has actual identity — you'd recognise a screenshot. Gold-as-data discipline keeps it honest. The risk is the opposite of V4: editorial demands content commitment. Right now V5 sits at ~70% of A24's air; for a draft companion it should compress to ~50%. Strength: identity, premium feel, fits the "we teach, not stat-dump" positioning.

What I'd do

The direction

Take V5's identity, push it to V4's density. The editorial palette + display/mono pair is the right brand. Compress row padding and shrink the display scale by ~30% so a phone shows 4–5 build rows on first scroll instead of 2–3. Keep the gold-as-data discipline.

Apply the Build Lab pattern — pickers on top, answer below — to every primary screen: home, hero detail, counters. Kill the "What do you need?" gate.

Don't keep V3. Retire V4's home once V5's home exists; keep V4's Build Lab for comparison until V5 covers all primary screens.

The fork — pick now

If you only adopt one thing from this review, it's this:

V5 → density-tuned

Editorial · Mustard · Compressed

Strongest identity, most ownable, fits "teach not dump" positioning. Needs density tuning before the full app rolls out.

V4 → polished

Esports · Glass · Refined

Safer, more conventional. Lower identity ceiling. Easier to ship without surprises.

When you've clicked through and have a gut answer, tell me the word that came to mind for V4, and the word for V5. That's almost always how to choose.
