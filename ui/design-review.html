The five lenses

Score each version 1–5 in your head against these. They translate directly to the audience's actual job — 30 seconds before a draft, on a phone, on the couch.

1. Audience fit
Does this feel made for a Predecessor player, or any MOBA tool?
2. Quick to digest
Can you understand the screen in 5 seconds without reading? Hierarchy doing the work?
3. Dense + meaningful
Every element earning its place — or decoration competing with data?
4. Linear flow, fewer taps
How many taps from "open" to the answer? Anything optional you can cut?
5. Identity (the bonus lens)
Could you describe the brand in one word after closing the tab? If yes — what's the word?

My honest take

V2 — Working baseline

Functional. Has every feature. Visually it could be any other MOBA tool — that's the problem. The home page works too hard with too many competing cards. Strength: completeness. Weakness: no identity, leans on win rates (which we want to drop).

V3 — Generic dashboard

Cleaner than V2 but doesn't earn its existence. Same conventions, slightly tidier. Verdict: retire it. Whichever direction you pick, V3 is not it.

V4 — Real personality, borrowed tropes

The glass + gradient + clip-cut tiles are well executed — but they're also what every esports tool from 2023 onward looks like. Cyan/violet says "futuristic gaming" without saying which game. The Build Lab proves the engine works; the visual treatment slightly fights the precision. Strength: familiar, gaming-fluent. Risk: dated quickly, easy to mistake for any other tool.

V5 — Distinctive point of view

The editorial restraint reads curated, not generated. Mustard on black has actual identity — you'd recognise a screenshot. Gold-as-data discipline keeps it honest. The risk is the opposite of V4: editorial demands content commitment — fewer items, more meaning. Right now V5's at ~70% A24's air; for a draft companion, compress to ~50%. Strength: identity, premium feel, fits "we teach, not stat-dump" positioning. Watch: doesn't sacrifice density.

What I'd do

Direction

Take V5's identity, push it to V4's density. The editorial palette + Anton/mono pair is the right brand. Compress the row paddings and shrink the display scale by ~30% so a phone shows 4–5 build rows on first scroll instead of 2–3. Keep the gold-as-data discipline.

Apply the Build Lab pattern (pickers on top → answer below) to every primary screen — home, hero detail, counters. Kill the "What do you need?" gate.

Don't keep V3. Retire V4's home once V5's home exists; keep V4's Build Lab for comparison until V5 covers all primary screens.

The fork — pick now

If you only adopt one thing from this review, it's this:

V5 → density-tuned

Editorial · Mustard · Compressed

Strongest identity, most ownable, fits "teach not dump" positioning. Needs density tuning before the full app rolls out.

V4 → polished

Esports · Glass · Refined

Safer / more conventional. Lower identity ceiling. Easier to ship without surprises.

When you've clicked through and have a gut answer, tell me the word that came to mind for V4 and the word for V5. That's usually how to choose.
