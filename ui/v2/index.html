ğŸ‘‹ About This Tool

Built by Kendrick (@saint_kendrick). Dad, gamer, tinkerer, mid lane main.

I came into Predecessor fresh after my Paragon buddies put me on. After getting more friends into the game, one thing was obvious: the learning curve is steep. Game timers, objectives, builds, matchups... there's a LOT to absorb. I started building tools for my friend group (a Discord bot for game timers, spreadsheets, you name it) and this is the next step.

I'm 400+ hours in and still Gold 1. Losing a match is tough, but it's what you take away from it that matters. That's the gap this tool is trying to fill: help you understand what went wrong, give you a heads up for what to look out for, and help you get better without needing to grind hundreds of hours first.

This isn't meant to replace sites like pred.gg. It's meant to supplement them. We want to go deeper: hero counters, matchup breakdowns, theorycrafting, team composition, and really diving into how augments and abilities come together to make or break a team outside of just the build.

It's also just fun. Come up with crazy team comps. Test builds that are unconventional or look terrible on paper. Learn something new. That's the point.

ğŸ“Š How It Works

Everything here is statistically derived from real match data. No opinions, no tier lists from streamers, no "trust me bro."

  • Win rates from thousands of real matches per hero, per role
  • Counter picks ranked by head-to-head win rates (20+ match minimum)
  • Builds are the highest win-rate item paths with enough games to be reliable
  • Augments show real win rates per choice, plus hand-curated playstyle notes
  • Hero profiles (tags, traits, archetypes) derived from ability data and curated for accuracy
  • Item tooltips with buy tips, counter tips, and synergy tags for every T3 item and crest

ğŸ” Data Source

Match stats scraped from pred.gg, which aggregates Predecessor's API. Re-analyzed to generate counter picks, build paths, and matchup insights that don't exist anywhere else.

Data refreshed each patch. Currently using all-ranks data for maximum sample size. Rank filtering is on the roadmap.

â“ FAQ

Why is [hero] missing data for [role]?
We skip roles with under 20 games. Bad data is worse than no data.

How are counter picks ranked?
Pure head-to-head win rate with a 20-match minimum. If Hero A beats Hero B 58% of the time across 200+ games, that's a counter.

What are the archetype tags?
Tags like "Burst Mage" or "Sustain Fighter" are derived from stats and abilities. Tap any tag for explanation and counter tips.

âš ï¸ Things to Be Aware Of

The data and tagging system are extensive but not perfect. Here's what to keep in mind:

  • CC shows up everywhere. Crowd control is the strongest counter to almost everything in Predecessor. If you see "has CC" as a reason a lot, that's not a bug. CC is king in this game.
  • Tags are hand-curated, not perfect. We manually classified 49 hero kits, 147 augments, and 188 items. If a tag feels wrong or a definition is off, let us know. Some edge cases are genuinely debatable.
  • All-ranks data. Currently using data across all ranks. A counter that stomps in Gold might not work the same in Diamond. Rank filtering is on the roadmap.
  • Win rates need context. A 60% win rate on 25 matches is less reliable than 53% on 500. We enforce a 20-match minimum, but smaller sample sizes still exist for niche picks.
  • Archetype tags are derived, not official. "Burst Mage" or "Sustain Fighter" come from our stat and trait analysis. Omeda doesn't label heroes this way. You might disagree with some classifications.
  • Augments can shift everything. A hero's base kit gets one set of tags, but augments can change their playstyle entirely. We account for this, but some augment interactions are complex and may not be fully captured yet.
  • Build Lab is new. The validation rules are solid but still growing. If it flags something that's actually fine (or misses something that's bad), that's feedback we want.

If you spot a tagging mistake, a definition that's off, or a counter that doesn't make sense, we want to hear about it. This tool gets better with community input.
